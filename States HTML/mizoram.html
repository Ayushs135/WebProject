Mizoram

The Land of the Hill People

Mizoram, nestled in the hills, is home to vibrant tribal traditions, bamboo dance (Cheraw), and scenic beauty. Festivals like Chapchar Kut celebrate nature and community life. Its culture thrives on music, handicrafts, and a strong sense of unity among its people.

Vantawng Falls

The highest waterfall in the state and the 13th highest in the country, the Vantawng Waterfalls is one of he most mesmerizing waterfalls of the North East. Vantawng falls are the pride of Mizoram and one of its prime attractions. The waterfall which is located 137 km away from the capital city of Aizawl is a gem in its own right. Tucked in between the verdant valleys with thick greenery around the waterfall seems like a river of white milk from distance.

Murlen National Park

Murlen National Park, located in Mizoram near the Myanmar border, is a biodiversity hotspot with dense forests, rare orchids, and diverse wildlife. Home to species like the Himalayan black bear and hoolock gibbon, the park is often called the “Amazon of Mizoram.” With scenic landscapes, caves, and waterfalls, it’s a paradise for nature lovers and adventurers.

Solomon Temple

Solomon's Temple in Aizawl, Mizoram, is a grand white-marble church built by the Holy Church. Established in 1996 and completed in 2017, it features stunning architecture with four towering pillars and intricate carvings. A major Christian pilgrimage site, it hosts thousands of worshippers and offers a serene atmosphere amidst lush green surroundings, making it a must-visit landmark.
