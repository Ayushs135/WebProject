Manipur

The Jewel of India

Manipur’s beauty lies in its lush landscapes, floating islands on Loktak Lake, and rich cultural heritage. Classical Manipuri dance, martial arts like Thang Ta, and festivals like Yaoshang reflect its artistic traditions. The blend of tribal and Meitei cultures shapes its vibrant identity.

Loktak Lake and Sendra Island

The largest fresh water lake in the country, Loktak Lake and the Sendra Island on it, are one of the most beautiful attractions of the state. Situated about 50 km from Imphal, Loktak Lake is situated in the valley of Imphal and is home to all the rivers and rivulets that run in the state of Manipur. Loktak Lake and Sendra Island present a combination of beauty unmatched anywhere else in the country.

Kangla Fort

Also known as the Palace of Kangla, this place situated on the banks of the Imphal river, served as the royal palace of King Pakhangba. After 2003 this fort has been open for public viewing. It has historical as well as archaeological significance which makes it a great tourist attraction.

Tharon Cave

Tharon Caves, located in Manipur’s Tamenglong district, are a fascinating network of limestone caves spanning around 655 meters. Rich in history, these caves feature ancient carvings linked to the Hoabinhian culture. Visitors can explore the dark passages, stunning rock formations, and mysterious chambers, making Tharon Caves a must-visit destination for adventure seekers and history enthusiasts.
