Jammu & Kashmir

The Crown of India

Nestled in the majestic Himalayas, Jammu & Kashmir is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, from snow-capped mountains to pristine valleys and serene lakes like Dal and Wular. The region is rich in Sufi traditions, vibrant Kashmiri handicrafts such as Pashmina shawls and carpets, and mouthwatering cuisine like Rogan Josh and Gushtaba. Festivals like Navroz and Hemis blend Buddhist and Islamic cultures, reflecting the region's diverse heritage.

Dal Lake

The Jewel of Srinagar, the Dal Lake is synonymous to a visit to Srinagar and is almost a visual definition of the town. Spread over 15 kms, it is the second-largest lake in Jammu & Kashmir. The mirror-like Dal Lake reflects the snow-dusted peaks of the Pir Panjal mountains while the colourful shikaras (gondolas) float around. The houseboats and shikaras are its main attractions.

Gulmarg

Situated at an altitude of 2730 m above sea level, Gulmarg is a popular skiing destination located in Pir Panjal Range of Union territory of Jammu and Kashmir. Surrounded by snow-covered lofty Himalayas, meadows of flowers, deep ravines, evergreen forested valleys, Gulmarg also has the world's second-highest Gondola ride.

Vaishno Devi

Vaishno Devi is a temple town that's home to the famous Vaishno Devi Mandir. Located in Trikuta hills, 13 kms from Katra (in the union territory of Jammu and Kashmir); this renowned shrine allures millions of devotees from all over the world. Popularly known as Mata Rani, Vaishnavi and Trikuta, Vaishno Devi is a manifestation of the Hindu Goddess Durga.
