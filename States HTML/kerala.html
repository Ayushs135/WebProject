Kerala

God’s Own Country

Blessed with lush backwaters, serene beaches, and misty hill stations, Kerala is a tropical paradise known for its unique culture and traditions. Kathakali and Mohiniyattam dance forms, the melodious Carnatic music, and the grand celebration of Onam reflect its artistic heritage. The state’s Ayurvedic wellness retreats and delectable cuisine, featuring appam with stew and seafood delicacies, add to its allure. Houseboat cruises in Alleppey and the tranquil tea plantations of Munnar make Kerala a haven for nature lovers.

Eravikulam National Park

Located a 15 minute drive away from Munnar, the Eravikulam National Park (also known as Rajamalai Wildlife Sanctuary), houses a rich and diverse variety of flora and fauna and is a UNESCO world heritage site. Elephant, Nilgiri langur, Nilgiri marten, Atlas moth (largest in the world), lion-tailed macaque, small-clawed otter, and a rare tiger or leopard are some of the main animal species that can be spotted here.

Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple

Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple, located in Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, is one of India’s most revered Hindu temples. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, it showcases Dravidian and Kerala architectural styles. The temple gained global fame for its hidden treasure vaults, making it one of the richest religious sites in the world. The deity, Lord Padmanabha, is depicted reclining on the serpent Anantha. Only Hindus are allowed inside, following strict dress codes.

Kerala Folklore Museum

Kerala Folklore Museum, located in Ernakulam, is a treasure trove of Kerala’s rich cultural heritage. Established in 2009, it showcases over 4,000 artifacts, including traditional masks, sculptures, musical instruments, and antiques. The museum’s architecture blends Malabar, Cochin, and Travancore styles. It also hosts live performances of classical dance and folk arts. A must-visit for history and art lovers!
