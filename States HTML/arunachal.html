Arunachal Pradesh

The Land of the Rising Sun

Arunachal Pradesh, with its rugged mountains and lush valleys, is a cultural mosaic of over 26 tribal communities. Its festivals like Losar and Torgya, traditional crafts, and vibrant dances reflect deep-rooted traditions. Pristine landscapes, monasteries like Tawang, and indigenous practices showcase the region’s unique heritage.

Tawang Monastery

Located amidst the picturesque surroundings, the Tawang Monastery also known as the Golden Namgyal Lhatse is one of the gems in Arunachal Pradesh. Perched at the height of 3,000 metres above sea level, it has been recognised as the second largest monastery in the world, the first one being Lhasa. The monastery is believed to be 400 years old and has control over 17 gompas in the region. Built as a large mansion, it is an abode for over 300 monks. Lying amidst the Himalayan ranges, it offers a breathtaking view of the Tawang-Chu valley. One of the most prominent attractions in the monastery houses is the massive statue of Lord Buddha which is 8 meters in height.

Daying Ering Wildlife Sanctuary

This sanctuary, and a couple of other facilities (school, hospital) in Pasighat are named after Dr. D. Ering. Over 25% of the sanctuary is covered with water bodies and hence attracts many migratory birds from Mongolia and Siberia. The sanctuary itsel is surrounded by the Siang River (Brahmaputra). the sanctuary is also home to many endangered species of birds.

Pasighat

Pasighat, the oldest town in Arunachal Pradesh, was established in 1911 and serves as the gateway to the state. Located along the picturesque Siang River, it offers stunning landscapes, lush greenery, and a rich cultural heritage. The town is home to the Adi tribe, known for their vibrant traditions and festivals like Solung and Aran. Adventure enthusiasts can enjoy river rafting, trekking, and angling. Pasighat also features attractions like Daying Ering Wildlife Sanctuary, which hosts migratory birds and diverse wildlife. With its blend of natural beauty and cultural richness, Pasighat is a must-visit destination in Northeast India.
